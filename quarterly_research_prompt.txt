## 1. Quarterly Result Summary

Explain how the company performed during the latest quarter. Analyze:

- Revenue
- EBITDA or operating profit
- EBITDA or operating margin
- Profit before tax
- Net profit
- EPS
- Cash flow, debt, and working capital where available

Compare every relevant metric:

- YoY: latest quarter versus the same quarter last year
- QoQ: latest quarter versus the immediately previous quarter

For every material change, explain why it happened. Clearly separate
management's explanation from your own analysis or inference.

## 2. Management Commentary

Analyze management's latest commentary from the earnings call, investor
presentation, result announcement, or stock-exchange filing.

Cover, where relevant:

- Demand and industry conditions
- Pricing and volume trends
- Input costs and margins
- Capacity utilization
- Capital expenditure
- Order book, deal wins, or pipeline
- New products and business segments
- Geographic performance
- Management guidance
- Risks and challenges

Identify any important change in management tone or guidance compared with the
previous quarter.

## 3. Business-Specific KPIs

First identify the KPIs that matter most for this company's industry and
business model. Analyze those KPIs YoY and QoQ.

Relevant KPIs may include volume, realization, capacity utilization, order
book, order inflow, customer additions, ARPU, same-store sales growth,
occupancy, assets under management, net interest margin, asset quality, credit
cost, deal wins, attrition, subscriber growth, or other industry-specific
measures.

Use only KPIs relevant to this company. For every material KPI change, explain
the reason using management commentary and reported disclosures. Write "not
disclosed" when a required KPI is unavailable.

## 4. Competitor Comparison

Identify the company's five closest listed competitors based on business
segments, product mix, customer market, and operating scale.

Before comparing performance, create a result-availability table covering all
five selected competitors. For each competitor show:

- Latest quarter expected or relevant for comparison
- Latest quarter actually reported
- Result status: Released, Not Yet Released, or Not Comparable
- Official source used to verify the status

Do not silently substitute an older quarter when the comparable latest result
has not been released. A competitor whose result is Not Yet Released or Not
Comparable must remain visible in the availability table but must be excluded
from performance rankings, peer averages, and peer medians.

For competitors with a released and comparable result, compare:

- Revenue growth
- EBITDA or operating-profit growth
- Margin movement
- Net-profit growth
- Management outlook

Also compare every company using the most important business-specific KPIs
identified in Section 3. Use the same KPI definitions across peers wherever
possible. For an IT-services company, for example, this should include relevant
measures such as constant-currency growth, total contract value or deal wins,
attrition, utilization, employee count, client additions, geographic growth,
and segment growth when disclosed. Select different KPIs when the company
belongs to another industry.

Present two Markdown tables:

1. Financial comparison with reporting period, revenue growth, operating-profit
   growth, margin and margin change, net-profit growth, and outlook.
2. Business KPI comparison with one row per company and columns for the most
   important comparable KPIs. Include YoY and QoQ movement for each KPI when
   disclosed.

For the KPI comparison:

- Explain which peer leads and lags on each important KPI.
- Compare {company_name} with the peer median or peer average where enough
  comparable data is available.
- Highlight whether each KPI gap is improving or worsening.
- Explain the operational reason behind material KPI differences using official
  management commentary.
- Mark unavailable or non-comparable values as "not disclosed" or "not
  comparable"; never estimate them.

State each competitor's reporting period and KPI definition. Do not compare
non-equivalent periods or differently defined KPIs without clearly disclosing
the difference.

Then explain:

- Which company delivered the strongest result?
- Which company showed the best growth and margin performance?
- Is {company_name} gaining or losing relative momentum?
- Is {company_name} gaining or losing ground on its most important business
  KPIs?
- What explains the major differences?

If fewer than five competitors have released comparable results, include only
those available in the financial and KPI comparison tables. Explicitly state
how many of the five peers were included and list the excluded peers with the
reason for exclusion.

If no competitor has released a comparable result, do not manufacture a peer
comparison. Show the availability table, state that a meaningful comparison is
not yet possible, and analyze {company_name} on a standalone basis.

If only one or two competitors have released comparable results, label the peer
sample as limited and avoid broad industry conclusions.

## 5. Positives and Concerns

List the most important data-supported positives and concerns from the latest

## 6. Overall Assessment

Classify the quarter as Strong, Positive, Mixed, Weak, or Not Comparable.
Explain the classification and state what should be monitored during the next

## Research Rules

- Use the most recent information available.
- Prioritize official company and stock-exchange sources.
- Cite the source and reporting period for important external claims.
- Treat the supplied structured data as authoritative for its reported fields.
- Do not invent missing figures, KPIs, commentary, competitors, or reasons.
- Clearly write "not disclosed" when information is unavailable.
- Distinguish reported facts, management explanations, and your own inference.
- Calculate changes from the supplied values when they are available.
- Round percentage changes to one decimal place.
- Express margin changes in basis points, where 100 basis points equals one
  percentage point.
- Avoid double-counting exceptional or one-time items.
- Explicitly say when a QoQ or YoY comparison is unavailable.
- Do not provide a buy, sell, hold, target price, or investment recommendation.
- End with: "This is an AI-generated result analysis, not financial advice."
